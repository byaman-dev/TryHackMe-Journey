# 📝 IMINT Notes

## 📖 What is IMINT?

Image Intelligence (IMINT) is the process of collecting and analysing information from photographs, satellite imagery, screenshots, and other visual sources.

Unlike traditional hacking techniques, IMINT relies on observation, critical thinking, and publicly available information to uncover intelligence.

---

# 🎯 Key Principles

- Observe before searching.
- Focus on small details.
- Correlate multiple clues.
- Verify findings using reliable sources.
- Never rely on a single piece of evidence.

---

# 🔍 Common Visual Clues

## Geography

- Mountains
- Rivers
- Coastlines
- Lakes
- Forests
- Desert landscapes

These features help narrow down possible locations.

---

## Buildings

- Architecture style
- Roof design
- Building materials
- Number of floors
- Historical landmarks

Architecture often indicates the country or region.

---

## Roads

- Road signs
- Road markings
- Traffic direction
- Highway numbers
- Street names

Road infrastructure varies between countries.

---

## Vehicles

- Registration plates
- Vehicle models
- Driving side (left/right)
- Emergency vehicles
- Public transport

Vehicle details provide valuable geographical clues.

---

## Environment

- Trees
- Vegetation
- Weather
- Climate
- Terrain

Natural features can eliminate many possible locations.

---

## Language

- Street signs
- Shop names
- Advertisements
- Road information

Languages quickly narrow the investigation.

---

## Shadows

Shadow direction can help estimate:

- Time of day
- Approximate direction
- Position of the sun

Useful during advanced investigations.

---

# 🧠 Investigation Tips

- Zoom into every part of the image.
- Search one clue at a time.
- Keep notes while investigating.
- Verify every assumption.
- Compare findings from multiple sources.

---

# ⚠ Common Mistakes

- Jumping to conclusions.
- Ignoring small details.
- Trusting one source only.
- Overlooking metadata.
- Assuming the obvious answer is correct.
