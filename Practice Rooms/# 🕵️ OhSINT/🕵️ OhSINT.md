\# 📖 About This Room



OhSINT is an introductory Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) challenge that demonstrates how publicly available information can be collected and analysed to answer investigative questions.



Rather than exploiting systems or using offensive security techniques, this room focuses on gathering intelligence from publicly accessible sources such as social media, search engines, metadata, and mapping services.



The room highlights the importance of digital footprints and demonstrates how seemingly harmless information can reveal valuable insights when combined through OSINT techniques.

\# 🔍 Investigation Methodology



Rather than relying on a single source of information, this room encouraged a structured investigative approach. Throughout the challenge, I followed a systematic methodology to collect, analyse, and validate publicly available information while documenting my findings.



\## Investigation Workflow



```text

Identify Available Evidence

&#x20;         │

&#x20;         ▼

Collect Public Information

&#x20;         │

&#x20;         ▼

Analyse Metadata \& Clues

&#x20;         │

&#x20;         ▼

Correlate Findings

&#x20;         │

&#x20;         ▼

Verify Information

&#x20;         │

&#x20;         ▼
```

This methodology demonstrates the importance of verifying information from multiple sources before drawing conclusions, a fundamental practice in both OSINT investigations and cybersecurity incident response.
